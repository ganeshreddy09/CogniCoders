The reality

55 million people live with dementia today.

Growing faster than any healthcare system can respond. Care is scarce. Attention is rationed. Families are left guessing in the dark.

The gap

Most only get a few hours of specialist care per week.

The other 165 hours — patients are without structured cognitive support. That's where decline accelerates, quietly and unnoticed.

The science

Daily stimulation slows the progression measurably.

Research shows consistent cognitive engagement — memory, sequencing, reminiscence — can delay decline and meaningfully improve quality of life.
